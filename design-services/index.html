Design Built for Production, Not Just Screens

There's a fundamental difference between design that looks good in a PDF and design that prints well. Color gamuts shift between RGB and CMYK. Fonts that render beautifully on screen can become unreadable at small sizes on paper. Bleed, trim, and safety zones matter when a blade cuts through your carefully placed headline.

VSL Print's design team understands production because they sit twenty feet from the presses. They know which paper stocks hold fine detail, which foils work on coated versus uncoated surfaces, and how to set up files that move through prepress without delays. That production knowledge saves you rounds of revision and prevents expensive reprints.

The Advantage of In-House Design

When your designer and your printer are in the same building, communication happens in real-time instead of through email chains. Our designers can walk to the press floor, pull a printed sample, and compare it to the screen. They can consult with press operators about ink coverage, talk to the bindery about spine widths, and check with finishing about foil compatibility — all before sending you a proof.

This integration eliminates the most common source of printing problems: files that weren't built for the production method. No more discovering that your photos need to be resampled, your blacks aren't rich enough, or your dieline doesn't fold correctly. We catch those issues in design, not on press.

File Preparation and Prepress

Already have designs from your agency or in-house team? We provide prepress file preparation services to ensure your files are production-ready. This includes preflight checking, color correction, trapping, imposition, and plate-ready output.

We work with all standard design formats — Adobe InDesign, Illustrator, Photoshop, PDF/X — and can accept files via email, cloud sharing, or direct upload to our FTP server. Detailed preflight reports identify any issues before your project enters production.
